NFTs Documentation

What are NFTs?

NFTs, or non-fungible tokens, are unique digital assets that are verified on a blockchain network. Unlike fungible tokens such as cryptocurrencies, NFTs cannot be exchanged for other tokens on a one-to-one basis as they have unique characteristics and cannot be replicated.

How do NFTs work?

NFTs are created on a blockchain network, typically using a smart contract, which defines the specific attributes and characteristics of the asset. The ownership and transfer of the NFT is recorded on the blockchain, which provides a transparent and immutable ledger of transactions.

Uses of NFTs

  • Collectibles
  • Gaming items
  • Virtual real estate
  • Artwork and music
  • Ticketing and event management

How to create and trade NFTs

To create an NFT, you need to first choose a blockchain platform that supports NFTs, such as Ethereum or Binance Smart Chain. You can then use a platform or service that allows you to mint your own NFTs, such as OpenSea or Rarible.

To trade NFTs, you can use a marketplace such as OpenSea or Nifty Gateway, where you can buy and sell NFTs with other users. Transactions are typically conducted using cryptocurrency such as Ether or Bitcoin.
